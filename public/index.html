Event Deep-Dive EngineHow this works ▾

Search any global event.

Type a conflict, policy, market move, geopolitical event — or any current-events topic. The engine pulls current sources and, where relevant, reads it through four lenses — financial, military, technological, and geopolitical — then maps its history, connections, and plausible futures.

The four lenses

Every event is read through up to four frames — Financial (who profits, capital flows), Military (spending, contracts, arms), Technological (surveillance, data, dual-use), and Geopolitical (alliances, leverage, sovereignty). Only the ones that genuinely fit are shown, each with a confidence rating.

The sourcing

Each deep-dive runs several searches from different angles — the event, the opposing case, the money trail, and non-Western coverage — then lists the real links it read, tiered by type: primary (official/data), reporting, analysis (think tanks), and reference. State-controlled outlets are flagged. Encyclopedias are a lead, not proof.

Both sides, you decide

It surfaces the strongest case for and against, names the empirical crux they actually disagree about, and — for deals — assesses how fragile it is. It does not hand down a verdict. Contested legal claims appear only as attributed allegations, never as asserted fact.

The honest limits

This is AI-generated and grounded in live web search. It can be wrong, out of date, or miss a source — confident wrong figures are the hardest to catch. Treat every claim as a lead to verify against the listed sources. Quick read is faster and lighter; Deep dive is the full analysis.
